Explore Our Workout Plans

1. Push Pull Legs (PPL)

Structure: Push (chest, shoulders, triceps), Pull (back, biceps), Legs

Frequency: 3–6 days/week

Best for: Balanced hypertrophy and strength, intermediate to advanced lifters

2. Bro Split (Body Part Split)

Structure: One muscle group per day (e.g., Chest, Back, Legs, Shoulders, Arms)

Frequency: 5 days/week

Best for: Bodybuilding, hypertrophy focus, intermediate lifters

3. Upper/Lower Split

Structure: Alternates Upper Body and Lower Body workouts

Frequency: 4 days/week (e.g., Upper, Lower, rest, Upper, Lower)

Best for: Strength and hypertrophy, all levels

4. Full Body Split

Structure: Each workout targets the entire body

Frequency: 2–4 days/week

Best for: Beginners, general fitness, fat loss

5. 5x5 (StrongLifts/Starting Strength)

Structure: Focus on 3 compound lifts per workout, 5 sets of 5 reps

Frequency: 3 days/week

Best for: Strength building, beginners to intermediates

6. German Volume Training (GVT)

Structure: 10 sets of 10 reps on major compound movements

Frequency: 3–4 days/week

Best for: Muscle mass and endurance, advanced lifters

7. PHUL (Power Hypertrophy Upper Lower)

Structure: Combines strength and hypertrophy with 2 Upper and 2 Lower days (Power + Hypertrophy)

Frequency: 4 days/week

Best for: Strength + size goals, intermediates

8. PHAT (Power Hypertrophy Adaptive Training)

Structure: Combines powerlifting and bodybuilding, 5 days/week

Frequency: 5 days/week (Power Upper/Lower + Hypertrophy Upper/Lower)

Best for: Advanced lifters, hybrid goals

9. Conjugate Method (Westside Barbell)

Structure: Max Effort and Dynamic Effort days for upper and lower body

Frequency: 4+ days/week

Best for: Strength athletes, powerlifters

10. 3-Day Split (Classic)

Structure: Often Chest/Triceps, Back/Biceps, Legs/Shoulders

Frequency: 3 days/week

Best for: General fitness, hypertrophy, time-constrained lifters

11. Bodyweight/Calisthenics Split

Structure: Skill + strength days, often Full Body, Push/Pull, or Upper/Lower format

Frequency: 3–6 days/week

Best for: Mobility, functional strength, minimal equipment training
